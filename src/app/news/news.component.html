  • August - Malaysia lifts all its Covid-19 entry restrictions

    All of Malaysia's Covid entry restrictions, such as testing and quarantine requirements, have been lifted for foreign arrivals. Prior to the regulation change, unvaccinated visitors were required to undergo a PCR testing two days prior to departure and a professionally administered rapid antigen test within 24 hours of arrival, followed by five days of quarantine. Tourists may still want to use the MySejahtera app as there is still uncertainty regarding the removal of the travel form.

  • The 833m-high Penang Hill (also known as Bukit Bendera) is located in the heart of the island and is popular with locals and tourists. It takes about three hours to reach the top, where the funicular train station is located. The trails up the hill are well maintained and have signboards, and there are several rest areas along the trail for walkers and hikers to take a breather. The most popular trails begin at either the Youth Park, Moongate, Botanical Gardens or Rifle Range Road. The Moongate trail is a popular hike, with the first two rest areas (No.3 and No.5) being the most popular.

    It takes 30 minutes to reach the first rest area (No.3) from the Youth Park, and 30 minutes to reach the next rest area (No.39). It takes 45 minutes to reach the top from No.84, and the cool air helps power through all the way. There is a mountain bike trail from the Youth Park, but many prefer to use the metal road. The funicular train is an interesting way to go up the hill.
